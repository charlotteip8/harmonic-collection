ingredients

* for pastry crust *

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1-2 tbsp cold water

* for egg custard filling *

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 tsp almond extract
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 tbsp cornstarch

Egg Tart (Dan Tat)

instructions

  1. In a bowl, sift together the flour, powdered sugar, and salt.
  2. Add the cubed butter into the dry ingredients. Cut the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles crumbs.
  3. Mix in the egg yolk, and then gradually add cold water until the dough begins to come together.
  4. Form the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  5. In a medium saucepan, combine the milk, heavy cream, and sugar. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is warm
  6. In a separate bowl, lightly beat the eggs. Add the vanilla and almond extracts, if using.
  7. Dissolve the cornstarch in a little bit of cold milk or water to make a smooth paste. Stir the cornstarch mixture into the warm milk mixture and continue heating while stirring constantly until the mixture thickens slightly
  8. Pour the warm milk mixture into the beaten eggs, whisking constantly to avoid curdling. Strain the mixture through a fine sieve to ensure a smooth, lump-free custard.
  9. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C), roll out the dough to about 1/8 inch thick. Use a round cutter (about 3-4 inches in diameter) to cut out circles of dough.
  10. Press each dough circle into the cups of a tart tin, gently pressing the dough into the sides and bottom to form a crust.
  11. Pour the egg custard mixture into each tart shell, leaving a little space at the edge to avoid spilling.
  12. Place the tarts on a baking sheet and bake in the preheated oven for about 15-18 minutes, or until the custard is set and the crust is golden brown
