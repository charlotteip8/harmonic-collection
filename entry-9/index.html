ingredients

  • 1 cup adzuki beans (red beans)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 cup sugar (or to taste)
  • Ice (crushed or shaved)
  • Sweetened condensed milk

    • * optional toppings *

  • Coconut milk
  • Taro balls
  • Fruit (like mango or lychee)
  • Mochi or sweet potato

Red Bean Ice (Hong Dou Bing)

instructions

  1. Rinse the red beans under cold water to remove any dirt.
  2. In a large pot, add the beans and 4 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until the beans are soft but not mushy.
  3. Once the beans are soft, drain any excess water.
  4. After draining the beans, return them to the pot. Add the sugar and stir until the sugar dissolves completely.
  5. Allow the beans to simmer for another 10-15 minutes to let the flavors meld together. Taste and adjust the sweetness if necessary.
  6. Let the beans cool down to room temperature.
  7. In a serving bowl or cup, fill it with shaved or crushed ice. You can use a blender to crush the ice or buy pre-shaved ice.
  8. Spoon a generous amount of the sweetened red beans over the ice.
  9. If you like, drizzle sweetened condensed milk over the beans and ice for a creamy touch.
  10. You can also top it with coconut milk for a richer flavor, or add other toppings such as mochi balls, taro balls, or pieces of fresh fruit like mango or lychee.
