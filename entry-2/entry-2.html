rice noodle rolls

Often sold at street vendors, HK style rice noodle rolls are made out of a rice flour batter that's steamed to create the perfect silky, chewy texture. Although these rice rolls are often stuffed with ingreedients such as shrimp or char siu (BBQ pork), the most nostalgic version to me are the plain rice rolls, topped with soy sauce, hoisin sauce, peanut sauce, and sesame seeds.

ingredients

For plain rice rolls

  • Rice flour
  • Corn starch
  • Tapioca starch
  • Wheat starch
  • Water

Instructions

  1. Put 1 cup of rice flour, 1 teaspoon of tapioca starch, 1 teaspoon of wheat starch and 1 teaspoon of cornstarch into a bowl.
  2. After that, pour 2 cups & 3 teaspoons of water into the bowl. Mix and stir it well.
  3. Next, brush some vegetable oil on a tray. Then, pour some rice flour mixture from step 2 into the tray.
  4. Then, steam it for 2 minutes after water is boiling. Repeat steps 2-4 a few times. It yields around 8 rice noodle rolls if you use the same size of the tray.
  5. In the meanwhile, put Â½ tablespoon of peanut butter in a container and add 1 tablespoon of water. Mix it very well until peanut butter is a little bit liquid.
  6. When the rice flour is done, let it cool down a little bit.
  7. Then, use a spatula to scrape the side. And, roll the rice noodle sheet up.
  8. Repeat steps 7 and 8 a few times. If you use the same size of the tray, you can make around 8 rolls. The following, use the spatula to cut rice noodle rolls into pieces.
  9. Lastly, add 1/2 tablespoon of sesame oil, 1/2 tablespoon of soy sauce, 1.5 tablespoons of hoisin sauce, some peanut sauce and sprinkle sesame seeds. (adjust the amount of sauce to your taste.)
